My journey into tech wasn't a straight line. Before I was building ETL pipelines and designing Data Warehouses, I was studying Pharmacy. I spent two years immersed in biology, chemistry, and pharmacology at Aljazeera University. But along the way, I realized that while I loved the analytical side of healthcare, my true passion was solving complex problems using data.

### The Pivot: From Medical Sciences to Data Science

The turning point came when I realized that data is the backbone of any modern decision-making process—whether it's in healthcare, business, or technology. I made the bold decision to switch my major to Data Science at the University of Hafr Al-Batin, and it was the best decision I ever made. 

My background in pharmacy actually gave me a unique advantage: a rigorous, detail-oriented mindset. In pharmacy, a small mistake in dosage can have huge consequences. I brought that same level of precision to data cleaning and data quality.

### Getting Hands-On with Data Engineering

During my studies, I quickly fell in love with the "plumbing" of data—how it moves, transforms, and gets stored. This led me to focus heavily on Data Engineering and Business Intelligence. 

I started working as a Data Engineer & BI Developer at the University of Hafr Al-Batin's Data Management Office, where I:
- Designed centralized Data Warehouses integrating academic and administrative data.
- Built ETL pipelines using **SSIS, SQL Server, and Python**.
- Developed interactive **Power BI dashboards** for university management.

Later, I joined **NexuMind** as a Data Engineering & Analytics Intern, scaling up to work on large-scale e-commerce data, building ML-assisted extraction models, and working with **PostgreSQL, NLP, and Metabase**.

### Building DataSense

One of my proudest achievements is developing **DataSense**, a full-stack adaptive data quality and dataset alignment platform. I built it using **React, Flask, REST APIs, and Docker** to solve a problem I faced firsthand: ensuring data quality and safe dataset synchronization across multiple sources. 

### Looking Ahead

Today, with a GPA of 3.991 (First Class Honors), I am focused on continuing my journey in Data Engineering, Big Data, and AI. My transition from pharmacy taught me that skills are transferable, and a diverse background is a strength, not a weakness.

If you're considering a career pivot into data, my advice is simple: **Start building.** Learn SQL, Python, and build projects that solve real problems. The data world needs diverse perspectives!
